Croeso!

I'm a graduate student in the Linguistics Department at the University of Arizona. My research centers on the Welsh langauge, particularly the variety spoken in Argentina. I'm interested in second language phonetics and phonology, bilingualism, language revitalization, and Celtic languages. This is the homepage for any and all of my currently running experiments.

If you're looking for my academic website, click here.

Experiments

For Welsh speakers

All experiments begin with a language background questionnaire, which takes around 5 minutes to complete. These experiments were designed in partnership with Skye Anderson, another graduate student at the University of Arizona.

The experiments may take up to 15 seconds to load. If you're having trouble loading an experiment, we recommend opening the link in a different browser. If you continue to have issues, feel free to contact me.

If you plan on completing more than one task, please enter the same initials at the beginning of each experiment, and feel free to skip the background questionnaire in subsequent experiments.

Note: these experiments are not accessible from mobile devices, please try them on a devise with an external keyboard (laptop or desktop computer).

  • Experiment 3: In this experiment, you will see a Welsh phrase, and hear a fake Welsh word at the same time. Then you'll answer a question about the word you heard by pressing a key. It will take less than 10 minutes to complete. Loading this experiment may take up to 15 seconds, we appreciate your patience!
